Eel rice

Unagi rice, also known as eel rice bowl, is one of the traditional summer foods in Japan. Unagi rice is a rice bowl made by dipping eel in a sweet and salty sauce, and then topping white rice with the side dish eel, also called eel rice bowl. The easiest season to taste is summer. This is because the 18 days before the beginning of autumn are called "Summer's soil use", which is the hottest period of the year. Since the Edo period, there has been "the day of the soil use ugly [around July 25th]. ] If you eat eel, you won’t lose weight in summer” because of the saying. soba:

Soba noodles contain 70% starch and 7%-13% protein, and the amino acid composition in the protein is relatively balanced, and the content of lysine and threonine is relatively rich. Soba noodles contain 2%-3% fat, and the content of oleic acid and linoleic acid, which are beneficial to the human body, is also high. These two fatty acids play a role in reducing blood lipids in the human body, and are also an important part of an important hormone-prostaglandin. The vitamins D1 and B2 in soba noodles are 3-20 times that of wheat flour, which are rare in general grains. The biggest nutritional feature of soba noodles is that it is rarely available in general food.

Tonkatsu:

Tonkatsu is a deep-fried pork dish found all over the world. Originated in Vienna, Austria. Austrian Vienna Pork Chops are bigger than a human face and are a must-have food in cafes. The practice is to flatten the pork to more than 70cm, soak it in champagne for a few hours, then wrap it in egg liquid, add bread crumbs, and fry it in a pan. The Shanghai fried pork chop is a very distinctive traditional snack.
